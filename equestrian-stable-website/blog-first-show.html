Preparing for Your First Show

Posted on July 22, 2024

Entering your first horse show is an exciting milestone! With proper preparation and the right mindset, you can make it a positive and memorable experience.

The key to a successful first show is preparation - both mental and physical. Here's your comprehensive guide to getting ready for competition day.

Choose the Right Show

Start with a local, low-key schooling show rather than a rated competition. Look for shows that offer beginner or walk-trot classes. Visit a show as a spectator first to understand the atmosphere and routine.

Read the prize list carefully and choose classes appropriate for your skill level. Don't be afraid to ask the show organizer questions!

Practice at Home

Simulate show conditions during your lessons. Practice entering and exiting the ring, halting at X for the judge, and maintaining composure in new situations. Work on your test or course until you can ride it from memory.

Have someone call out your test or course while you ride. This helps you learn to listen while maintaining focus on your horse.

Prepare Your Horse

Ensure your horse is comfortable with the show environment. Practice loading and unloading from the trailer. Expose your horse to new sights and sounds. A calm, well-prepared horse makes for a more enjoyable experience.

Schedule a bath and grooming session the day before. Clean and check all your tack. Make sure shoes are secure and hooves are trimmed.

What to Pack

For Your Horse: Halter, lead rope, water buckets, hay net, grooming supplies, hoof pick, fly spray, first aid kit, and any medications.

For You: Show clothes, boots, helmet, gloves, number, whip if allowed, water, snacks, sunscreen, and a chair for waiting.

Tack: Saddle, bridle, girth, saddle pad, and any additional equipment needed for your class.

Show Day Tips

Arrive early to allow time for check-in, warm-up, and unexpected delays. Walk the show grounds with your horse before your class. Stay hydrated and eat light, healthy snacks.

Remember to breathe! Nerves are normal. Focus on riding your best and enjoying the experience rather than winning. Every rider was a beginner once.

After the Show

Win or lose, celebrate your achievement! Take time to reflect on what went well and what you'd like to improve. Thank your horse, your trainer, and anyone who helped you prepare.

Most importantly, have fun! The show ring is where all your hard work comes together.
