5 Tips for Better Balance

Posted on October 12, 2026

Balance is the foundation of all good riding. Without it, your aids become unclear, and you risk unsettling your horse.

Here are five essential tips to help you improve your stability in the saddle:

1. Check Your Stirrup Length

Riding with stirrups that are too long or too short can severely impact your position. Ensure your stirrup hangs just below your ankle bone when your feet are out of the irons.

2. Looking Ahead

Looking down at your horse drops your center of gravity forward. Keep your eyes up and focus on where you are going.

3. Work Without Stirrups

Lunge lessons without stirrups are excellent for deepening your seat and finding your natural balance.

4. Engage Your Core

A strong core supports your back and allows your hips to follow the horse's movement independently.

5. Breathe

Tension blocks movement. Remember to breathe rhythmically to keep your body relaxed and supple.
